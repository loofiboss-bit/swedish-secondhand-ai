# Choose a price

You make the final price decision. The app supports three routes and keeps their labels separate.

## Use your own price

Choose **Use my own price** when you already know the amount or want the shortest workflow. The
app records the value as **Your price**. It does not add a confidence score, price range, or claim
that AI or market evidence supports it.

## Decide later

Choose **Decide later** when you want to prepare the text before pricing. You can review and copy
basic listing text, but the complete structured package remains blocked until the project has a
price.

## Use evidence-based pricing

A numeric recommendation requires at least two comparable sales that meet both conditions:

1. the price is a verified realized price, not an asking price or unknown price type;
2. you explicitly approve the comparable for this item.

The app ranks and weights eligible comparables, shows exclusions, and applies deterministic
adjustments. You can inspect the fast-sale, balanced, and max-value scenarios. If fewer than two
approved realized prices remain, the result is `insufficient-evidence` and contains no numeric
recommendation.

## Read comparable prices correctly

- **Realized price** means a recorded final sale price. It can support a recommendation after you
  approve it.
- **Asking price** means the amount in an active listing. It appears only as market context.
- **Unknown price type** cannot support a recommendation until you verify what the amount means.

Check the item match, condition, included parts, sale date, delivery terms, and source before
approval. Exclude a comparable when the item differs in a way that changes value.

## Add a manual comparable

Choose the source, record the observed date and price, and mark whether it is asking, realized, or
unknown. Add the source URL when available. A manual entry does not become trusted evidence merely
because it has a number; verify the price type and approve it deliberately.

## Use the recommendation

Review every visible adjustment before choosing a scenario. The recommended amount is a starting
point, not a guarantee of sale. Marketplace fees, shipping, season, local demand, and changes in
item condition can affect the result after the observation date.
